The Air Quality Observations report contains average air quality readings for major metropolitan and smog-prone areas around the United States and Canada for the past 24 hours. Many stations only report during the summer months. The information contained within this report is gathered by the EPA as part of their AirNow project. The US air quality report is transmitted once per day in the evening (Pacific Time).

AIR QUALITY CONDITIONS

G=Good

M=Moderate

S=Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups

U=Unhealthy

V=Very Unhealthy

H=Hazardous

airqconcentration

0-50 = Good

51-100 =Moderate

101-150 = Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups

151-200 = Unhealthy

201-300 = Very Unhealthy

>300 =Hazardous

DESCRIPTION

Minimal =0

A UV Index reading of 0 means very low danger from sun’s UV rays, wearing a hat is sufficient protection.

Low =1-2

A UV Index reading of 1 to 2 means low danger from the sun's UV rays for the average person.

Moderate = 3-5

A UV Index reading of 3 to 5 means moderate risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure.

High = 6-7

A UV Index reading of 6 to 7 means high risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure. Protection against skin and eye damage is needed.

Extreme = 8-10

A UV Index reading of 8 to 10 means very high risk of harm from unprotected sun exposure. Take extra precautions because unprotected skin and eyes will be damaged and can burn quickly.
